I think the most pertinent lesson of today is that not everything goes according to schedule. This isn't to say that I have renegged on my endeavour whatsoever, in fact it's quite the opposite. I was able to accomplish my goals for to today in sub-optimal conditions which I feel is worth note. With the exception of running (due to time constraints) I hit every target area for today.

For the sake of logistics I believe I'll structure these posts according to the following criteria:

  1. Briefing
  2. Target areas of study/performance
  3. Explanations/Challenges

For today, I decide on touching the following activities:

  • Drawing (In this case working through Loomis' Fun with a pencil.
  • Japanese vocab (hiragana practice)
  • Onset detection.

For each I dedicated 30 minutes to one hour of practice. Although for anyone seriously interested in any of the above, this may not seem sufficient, one must remember that I plan on working at this for a significant amount of time.

The first two activities completed uneventfully, however I am at this point highly interested in the topic of onset detection and in a slightly broader scope, signal processing. I've worked with the concept before in a game-design class in order to make a rhythm reliant game, however the game itself was hacky and didn't touch the topic in a depth that I felt satisfied with. Onset detection will most likely be a recurring topic for my study. The actual implementation of this is yet to be decided.

On a somewhat personal note, I'm glad this project didn't immediately fizzle as many of my shower thoughts do.
