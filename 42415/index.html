Piano is en-route!

Finally pulled the trigger on a new digital piano. The one in particular I settled on is a Williams Legato 88-key digital piano. For the price range it seems like a good choice. The form factor simple and elegant while and also has including any features that I feel would be necessary as a beginner. Looking forward to including my endevaours with that into these posts.

As for today...I continued learning from the Genki book. I haven't begun yet on the workbook content but I'll probably get to that tomorrow. As for what I actually learned....a mish mash of things to be honest. This chapter wentt over a lot of situational content. For example, when being unsure about what you're about to say one would usually say ano (あの). The main thing I focsed on today was the numbering system which is simple enought o learn. From one to ten the order goes (in romaji): ichi. ni, san. yon, go, roku, nana, hachi, kyuu, juu. From 10+ you append X-juu(number) to form the number. So for example 27 would be ichijuu-nana. This pattern remains the same until 100+, in which I'm completely unfamiliar.

Additionally other tidbits like using the particle no (ね) can be used a either a seperator for telephone numbers or a common way to assign ownership in a sentence.

I feel like I am making slow but steady progess. Hopefully 4 years from now I'll enjoy the results of my work.
